# Case → Analysis → Implication / 사례 → 분석 → 시사점 (Case → Analysis → Implication)

- English name: Case → Analysis → Implication
- Korean name: 사례 → 분석 → 시사점 (Case → Analysis → Implication)
- Selection category: case
- Use when: Use when a concrete case can carry the argument and lead to broader implications.
- Avoid when: Avoid when there is no specific case or evidence to inspect.

## Writing Pattern

**Use this fixed three-stage structure**

- Case
  - Present a real incident or a case from a company/service/organization.
  - Describe background, developments, and outcomes concretely.
- Analysis
  - Identify and interpret the key factors, causes, and impacts embedded in the case.
  - Use figures, diagrams, and comparisons to build persuasion.
- Implication
  - Derive what this case means for the topic and reader now.
  - Turn it into actionable insight: strategic takeaways or practical applications.

## Example Writing Outline

1. **Branding Success at Startup A**: By expanding social viral touchpoints, brand awareness quadrupled in six months. (Case)
2. **Analyzing the Success Factors**: A well-defined customer persona and emotionally resonant messaging were central; timing of the rebrand was crucial. (Analysis)
3. **Implications for Brand Growth**: At the early stage, branding that clarifies identity can be the key to loyalty over raw performance. (Implication)

## Writing Notes

- Write the Case concretely in a storytelling style the reader can easily follow; for famous cases, emphasize objectivity and fresh insight.
- In Analysis, go beyond description—compare factors and focus on causal interpretation; examples, comparisons, or tables strengthen credibility.
- For Implication, move past moral-of-the-story; propose specific insights or strategies the reader can apply.
- Maintain the chain: “Why is this case important? → What essence does it reveal? → What strategic meaning does it hold for us?”
