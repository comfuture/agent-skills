# 3 Key Points / 핵심 3가지 포인트 강조 (3 Key Points)

- English name: 3 Key Points
- Korean name: 핵심 3가지 포인트 강조 (3 Key Points)
- Selection category: synthesis
- Use when: Use when the draft needs three balanced pillars, principles, or claims.
- Avoid when: Avoid when the material is mainly causal, chronological, or conflict-driven.

## Writing Pattern

**Keep the major-section sequence fixed; build around this flow**

- Intro / Summary of the overall message
- Key Point 1
- Key Point 2
- Key Point 3
- Recap / Closing message
- Each key point should live on its own section but remain organically connected to the overall message.

**Every major section should include**

- Section heading
- Core paragraph purpose (1–2 sentences)
- When helpful, suggest supporting evidence or memorable details for each point (keywords, brief case notes, quotes, examples, or data points.).

## Example Writing Outline

1. **Why Change Is Needed Now**: With competition intensifying, differentiated strategy is more necessary than ever.
2. **Key Point 1 – Customer-Centric Strategy**: By incorporating real-time customer feedback, we shortened our product improvement cycle.
3. **Key Point 2 – Technological Innovation**: AI-driven automation improved operational efficiency by 40%.
4. **Key Point 3 – Flexible Culture**: A culture that values autonomy and accountability is boosting talent retention.
5. **Conclusion and Expected Impact**: These three core strategies form the foundation for our sustained growth.

## Writing Notes

- Keep the three points equally important, clearly distinct, and free of overlap or vagueness.
- Reinforce each point with concrete cases or data to increase credibility.
- Though simple on the surface, the narrative should communicate strategic depth.
- Make the message powerful and concise so the reader feels “three are enough.”
