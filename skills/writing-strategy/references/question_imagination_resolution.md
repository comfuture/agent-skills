# Question → Imagination → Resolution / 반응 유도형 (질문 → 상상 → 정리)

- English name: Question → Imagination → Resolution
- Korean name: 반응 유도형 (질문 → 상상 → 정리)
- Selection category: creative
- Use when: Use when a speculative question or what-if scenario can open the reader's imagination.
- Avoid when: Avoid when the topic requires strict factual reporting.

## Writing Pattern

**Use the following three-step flow**

1. Pose a question
2. Expand with imagination or a scenario
3. Writer’s synthesis and core message

**Each major section should include**

- Section heading
- Core paragraph purpose (1–2 sentences)
- Core paragraph purposes should guide the reader’s thinking and connect imagination and interpretation smoothly.

## Example Writing Outline

1. **What If This Happened to You?**: If you had to wait over 20 minutes to reach support, how would you feel?
2. **Picture This**: Frustration, distrust, and churn—customers won’t wait.
3. **What We Changed**: We introduced a chatbot and cut response time by 90%. Customers don’t have to wait anymore.

## Writing Notes

- The question should evoke empathy and relevance—use realistic situations and emotions.
- The imagination stage should provide a concrete scenario to deepen immersion.
- In the final synthesis, resolve the raised issue or propose a new direction with a clear message.
- Because this structure blends storytelling and message reinforcement, the flow and emotional arc matter greatly.
